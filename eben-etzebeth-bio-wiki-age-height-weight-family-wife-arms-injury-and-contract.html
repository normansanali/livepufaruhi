Eben Etzebeth Rugby Player

Etzebeth is a professional rugby union player. Currently, he plays for the South Africa national team and Toulon in the Top 14 in France. Etzebeth was marked out as a player of huge promise from an early age when playing for Hoërskool Tygerberg in Cape Town, and he entered the Western Province youth structures in the year 2009, featuring in the Under-18 Craven Week tournament.

In the year 2011, his career moved up a gear when he was part of the UCT Ikey Tigers side which won the Varsity Cup, however, injury stalled his progress in the second half of the year, and he was unable to play any part in the year 2011 Currie Cup.

His injury did not stop him from being named in the Stormers squad for the year 2012 Super Rugby season. He recovered from injury in time for the start of the campaign and on 25 February 2012, he debuted against the Hurricanes. In total, he made 13 appearances during the season and managed to score 1 try as the Stormers won 14 of their 16 league matches prior to eventually losing to the Sharks in the semi-finals.

International commitments kept him out of the early stages of the year 2012 Currie Cup, but he went back at the tail-end of the tournament, winning his first 3 Western Province caps and helping them to lift their first Currie Cup title since the year 2002. He was awarded the Man of the Match award as Province gained revenge on the Sharks with a 25–18 win in Durban.

Top 14 side Toulon reported Etzebeth would join them after the 2019 Rugby World Cup in December 2018. The injury ruled him out of the first half of the year 2013 Super Rugby season, but he returned for the second. He made a start in January 2021, at Blindside Flank for the first Time after teammate Swan Rebbadj got injured prior to kick off.
