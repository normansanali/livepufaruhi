Bernard William Nussbaum is a well-known American lawyer who served as White House Counsel under President Bill Clinton.

Nussbaum, who worked on the House Judiciary Committee’s impeachment inquiry of President Richard Nixon, advised Clinton not to hire a special counsel to investigate the Whitewater scandal in the early 1990s.

Clinton disobeyed his counsel, a crucial choice that laid the groundwork for the Starr investigation.

How Old Is Bernie Nussbaum? His Age Details

The current age of Bernie Nussbaum is 84 years old.

He was born on March 23, 1937, in  New York City, U.S. His father and mother used to work in the clothing industry.

His father later worked for the International Ladies’ Textile Workers’ Group, a labor union that represented garment workers (ILGWU).

Nussbaum grew up on Manhattan’s lower east side, which at the time was home to a large number of Eastern European immigrant families.

He went to the public schools in his neighborhood. Then, in 1951, he was accepted to Stuyvesant High School in New York City, a specialist public high school that requires students to pass an entrance exam.

He graduated in 1954, having served as an editor on the school’s newspaper, the Stuyvesant Spectator, and as a member of the school’s academic honor society.

Bernie Nussbaum Wife Nancy Kuhn

Bernie Nussbaum married his wife Nancy Kuhn, a fundraiser for political and philanthropic groups, in December 2008.

She played a key role in raising cash for Democratic presidential candidate Walter Mondale in New York State and abroad during the 1984 presidential campaign.

Nussbaum and Kuhn lived in New York City, Stamford, Connecticut, and Naples, Florida. Nancy died in April 2021 from brain cancer.
