Where Do Mobile Mechanics Work?

While mobile mechanics are awesome in that they come to you, there are a few things to consider in order to provide a safe and efficient work environment.

For instance, it’s best if your vehicle is parked on a paved surface that is relatively flat to allow the mechanic to safely and easily maneuver under it, if needed. If you have access to a garage or carport, parking your vehicle there for service is preferable. A driveway or parking lot will also work. Ideally, you want to avoid parking your vehicle in the street, on a steep hill, or on an unpaved surface, for safety reasons.

Keep in mind that most mobile mechanics arrive in trucks or vans that carry all their tools and equipment. Our mobile mechanics at AutoNation Mobile Service arrive in fully-equipped company vans. Ideally, they’d be able to park their vans as close as possible to the vehicle that needs service.

This might be a bit tricky if you live in an apartment complex with limited parking. Try your best to locate a safe area where the van can park nearby. For those who live in gated communities, remember you’ll need to provide the van access as well.

While most repairs can be performed at your home or office, there are some jobs that require a shop with a lift. For such repairs, AutoNation Mobile Service has a network of certified shops ready to provide assistance.

If you’re not sure which services you need, you can describe the issue to the best of your ability – noting any bizarre noises, leaks, or other symptoms you might have noticed. You can also choose to speak directly with one of AutoNation Mobile Service’s expert service advisors over the phone.

Once you select a day and time that works best for you, we will dispatch a mechanic to your location of choice. The mechanic will arrive in a company van, equipped with all the tools and parts necessary to get the job done. To ensure that they show up with the correct parts, it’s important that you give accurate information about your vehicle. For example, providing your VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) can go a long way in helping the mechanic secure the correct parts.

Common Questions Before You Select a Mobile Mechanic

What Qualifications Or Certifications Do Your Mechanics Hold?

There are plenty of exceptional mechanics around that don’t hold certification but shops that prioritize hiring certificated and professionally trained mechanics indicate a workshop is prepared to give their employees work their stamp of approval. If a mechanic is lacking certification it doesn’t mean they are incompetent, but it is one aspect to consider when choosing a mobile mechanic with the both the knowledge and skills required to fix your car.

 Will You Provide An Estimate?

Nobody likes getting their car back along with a giant bill so providing an estimate can help avoid unpleasant financial surprises. An estimate also allows you to compare prices between shops if you so desire, and discuss any discrepancies over price.

 Do You Guarantee Your Work?

For complete peace of mind, your mechanic should offer a warranty on the work they do. This indicates that they use high quality parts, are part of a team that hires competent technicians and that they value their customers. A business that offers a warranty is an indication they have full confidence in the service they provide.

Are Mobile Mechanics Cheaper than Repair Shops?

You may be wondering whether a mobile mechanic or a repair shop is going to cost you less. Additionally, which offers better value and how can you choose between the two? The obvious difference between mobile mechanics and repair shops is that repair shops operate out of a brick and mortar workshop and will have higher overheads and expenses than a mobile mechanic.

Not being tied to a physical location is a huge cost benefit but mobile mechanics offer a more streamlined process that requires less staff as well. When you call a traditional repair shop you may first speak to a receptionist, who then puts you through to someone to take your booking. When you drop off your vehicle a mechanic will work on it, a workshop foreman or supervisor will sign off on the work once it’s finished before another staff member prepares your invoice. Mobile workshops will typically hire experienced staff that don’t need supervision and can perform more than one job, greatly cutting down on labor and staffing costs.

So while mobile mechanics have lower operating costs which are passed onto their customers, making them cheaper than repair shops, price alone shouldn’t be the deciding factor when choosing who to trust to fix your car. Instead, it can be used alongside other considerations like customer reviews and warranty comparisons.

What Kind of Warranties Do Mobile Mechanics Offer?

Being an informed consumer when it comes to warranties can protect you from parts failure or workmanship that is not up to par. But even if you have a mechanic that you know and trust, it’s nice to know you and your vehicle are protected by a warranty on the off-chance that something does go wrong.

It’s unfortunate that most people don’t know their rights when it comes to automotive repair. In most cases if a repair is carried out that causes problems to the vehicle or is substandard, a lawsuit should be made within 2 years of the problem occurring, and that is after you have sent a written demand to the repair shop by certified mail within the first 60 days. Even after all that, you are only covered for up to $10,000 – far less than the value of your vehicle.

As you can see, if your mechanic doesn’t offer a warranty as part of their service and something goes wrong, you could be out of pocket for thousands of dollars, and a claim against implied warranty can take months to settle in court. Avoid the potential hassle and protect your vehicle by always choosing a mechanic that can back up their work with a guarantee – like our own 12,000 mile / 12 month warranty policy.
