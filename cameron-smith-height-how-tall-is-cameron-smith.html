Cameron Smith career

Smith competed on the PGA Tour of Australasia after turning pro in 2013. At the 2016 Emirates Australian Open and the 2015 Coca-Cola Queensland PGA Championship, he tied for second place.

In 2014, Smith participated in the Asian Tour. He had seven top-10 finishes and placed fifth overall on the Order of Merit. At the 2014 CIMB Niaga Indonesian Masters, he tied for second place, which was his best result.

Smith finished tied for fifteenth in the RBC Heritage in April while using a sponsor’s exemption. His top-4 finish earned him an entry to the 2016 Masters Tournament after he qualified for the 2015 U.S. Open.

Smith received Special Temporary Membership on the PGA Tour for the balance of the 2015 season in addition to the victory.

Smith qualified for the 2015–16 PGA Tour by earning enough money as a non–member to have finished in the top 125 on the money list, which would have required him to play in his best three events.

Smith was 157th on the FedEx points standings in 2016. He was able to return to the PGA Tour in 2017 because to his success in the Web.com Tour Finals, where he finished second in the Nationwide Children’s Hospital Championship.

The Zurich Classic of New Orleans was won by Smith and Jonas Blixt in May, marking the first team event on the PGA Tour since 1981. The pair defeated Scott Brown and Kevin Kisner in a playoff and didn’t make a bogey the entire tournament.

Smith successfully defended his title at the Australian PGA Championship in December, defeating Marc Leishman by two shots. Smith participated in the 2019 Presidents Cup at the Royal Melbourne Golf Club in Australia in December as a member of the International squad.

Smith achieved his first individual success on the PGA Tour in January when he defeated Brendan Steele in a playoff to win the Sony Open in Hawaii.

Smith captured the Zurich Classic of New Orleans for a second time in April. He teamed up with fellow countryman Marc Leishman this time. In a playoff, they defeated Louis Oosthuizen and Charl Schwartzel.

Smith triumphed at the Kapalua Resort in Maui, Hawaii, in the 2022 Sentry Tournament of Champions in January.

Smith defeated world number one Jon Rahm by one stroke thanks to a PGA Tour record-breaking 34-under-par round. The previous record, achieved by Ernie Els in 2003 at the same tournament, was 31 under par. 34 under par shattered that mark.

At the 150th Open Championship, held at the Old Course at St. Andrew’s, in July, Smith won his maiden major title.

He finished one shot ahead of Cameron Young and two shots ahead of joint third-round leader Rory McIlroy after shooting a final-round 64 to recover from being four strokes off the lead.
