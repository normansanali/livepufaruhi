Arthur Blank was first married to Diana J. Blank. They have three children named Danielle Blank, Dena Blank Kimball, and Kenny Blank. The couple divorced in 1993. Career

After completing his college, Arthur Blank used to work in the Arthur Young and Company as a senior accountant. Following that, he started to work at the Daylin Corporation. Later he became the president of Daylin’s Elliott’s Drug Stores/Stripe Discount Stores. Blank then moved to the division of Handy Dan Home Improvement Centers.

In 1978, he became the co-founder of Home Depot, along with Bernard Marcus. It is a company that sells construction products, tools, and services and is headquartered in Atlanta Store Support Center. It soon revolutionized the home improvement business and started to evolve the new concept of warehousing. This led both Blank and Marcus to become billionaires.

For 19 years, Arthur Blank served as the president of the company, and following that, he became the CEO of the company. In 2001, he retired from the company as its co-chairman.

In 2002, he bought National Football League’s Atlanta Falcons. Two years later, he purchased the Arena Football League franchise, Georgia Force. He is also the owner of Atlanta United FC, which plays in Major League Soccer.

Awards & Achievements

In 2011, the American Institute for Stuttering conferred Arthur Blank with the Changing Lives Award. In 2006, he was inducted into the Junior Achievement U.S. Business Hall of Fame. He was named as the National Entrepreneur of the Year by Ernst & Young LLP in 2005. Georgia Trend magazine has named Blank as Georgia’s Most Respected CEO. The Georgia Historical Society inducted Arthur Blank as a Georgia Trustee in 2014.
