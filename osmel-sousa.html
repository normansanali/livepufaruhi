Introduction

Osmel Sousa, nicknamed “The Tsar of Beauty,” is a beauty pageant entrepreneur.

Biography

He was born on September 26, 1946, in Cienfuegos, Cuba, as Osmel Ricardo Sousa Mansilla.

At age 13, his family sent him to live with his grandmother in Venezuela.

”I had non-male gestures, and I think that’s why they sent me to Venezuela,” Osmel explained in a later interview. He added: ”They put me on hormone treatment and all they did was ruin my voice.”

In 1969, Osmel started to work with the Venezuelan Committee of Beauty. 

In 1981, he was appointed director of the Miss Venezuela pageant. ”I say that inner beauty doesn’t exist,” Sousa once said. He continued: ”That’s something that unpretty women invented to justify themselves.”

Osmel Sousa and Aylin Mujica are seen at Telemundo’s “Los 50” VIP preview event – @Getty

Sousa became the franchise manager for Mister World for Venezuela in 1996.

In 2007, Osmel joined the judging panel of Univision’s Hispanic beauty contest ”Nuestra Belleza Latina.” He was accompanied by a former Miss Universe, Lupita Jones.

Retirement

On February 6, 2018, he made a shocking revelation when he announced he was quitting Miss Venezuela.

”Today, I’ve decided to leave the presidency of the Miss Venezuela Organization through the big door, the same door that I came through almost 40 years ago by the hand of my great friend Gustavo Cisneros,” Sousa wrote on his Instagram.

Jennifer Lopez & Osmel Sousa – © Guliver / Getty Images

He was succeeded by Gabriela Isler (Miss Universe in 2013) as a national director for Venezuela.

On April 9, 2019, Osmel started his self-entitled YouTube channel (Osmel Sousa TV). At thew time of writing, it has over 93k subscribers.

In July 2019, it was announced that Osmel will be the director of the beauty contests of Miss Uruguay and Miss Argentina.

In 2021, Osmel confirmed his resignation as Miss Universe Argentina National Director.

Wife

Osmel Sousa is not married. Also, there is no information about his girlfriend.

Sousa said about the gay movement, child adoption, and marriage:

”I don’t agree with gay marriage, nor with gays adopting children. I would never marry, nor would I ever adopt children.”

Osmel went on to say:

”If you want to help a child, you give money, you sponsor him completely until he grows up, but dad and dad mmm — that’s not on my mind, nor is mom and mom either.”
